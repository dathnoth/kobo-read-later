The News

Victoria to make it easier to "demask" anonymous posters

Victoria's Premier put out a vague press release yesterday saying that "Labor will give VCAT new powers to make 'demasking orders' – forcing social media companies to reveal the identity of anonymous users accused of online vilification". If I squint I can see some well meaning intent here, but the unintended consequences of a law like this really depend on the legislation. At the very least I hope the threshold for "demasking" someone is very high. I can easily imagine a scenario where someone with a legitimate gripe about say, union thugs on Melbourne building projects, is "demasked" so the government can make an example of them. Premier Allen is also cooking up a law that'll let you sue "a social media or AI company for negligence causing psychiatric harm to their child" without requiring "proof of a permanent impairment of 10% or more".

More federal government noises in the direction of AI

Six federal ministers put out a joint "AI safety priorities" press release with some brain farts and old announcements on AI legislation. There's plans to beef up consumer law to protect us from "retail surveillance pricing and agentic commerce", a "framework to better regulate the use of automated decision-making within federal agencies" and something about "AI Safety in the Workplace" - which I think is about making sure dumb bosses don't cram AI into every aspect of their business to the detriment of employees? There's also some rehashing of the much awaited "second tranche of privacy reform" from the Attorney-General and the even longer awaited "Digital Duty of Care" from the comms minister. As with the Victorian government's brain farts, I'll reserve judgement until there's some details. Until then I'm optimistically skeptical.

LG monitors sneakily install software without a user's permission

Looks like some LG monitors "automatically installs software that promotes McAfee subscriptions" when plugged into a Windows 11 PC. From the article: "Windows Update first installed LG extension and software component packages. Windows Reliability Monitor showed that LG Monitor App Installer appeared one minute later. The installation did not display a consent prompt or require the user to approve the download". It does all this with no user interaction as the software is delivered via Windows Update. What a shitty thing to do without the consent of the computer's owner and what a shitty thing Microsoft allows hardware manufacturers do. Check out this 30min video from Gamers Nexus for a full demo of LG's sneaky software in action.

Something I Saw On The Internet

Mozilla says Euki is a good period tracking app

Mozilla did a privacy test of six popular period tracking apps - Euki, Clue, Flo, Period Calendar, Planned Parenthood's Spot On, and Stardust - and found that Euki was the standout choice. It doesn't have "social features, no cross-device backup, and no need to create an account" and they "didn't find anything in our tests to suggest there was anything extra going on behind the app's minimal setup. Even when we opened external pages in the in-app browser, none of the health information we entered, like cycle data or symptoms, left the app". Thank you Mozilla for doing this important work.
