The News

OpenAI's internal evaluation of GPT-5.6 Sol went rogue

OpenAI has made public that Hugging Face - a popular LLM community/marketplace/I don't really know - got hacked by their own agent that was supposed to be running in an OpenAI sandbox to avoid this kinda thing. The LLM "identified and chained vulnerabilities across OpenAI's research environment and Hugging Face's production infrastructure to obtain test solutions directly from Hugging Face's production database", i.e: the LLM managed to successfully escape the sandbox by using a zero-day vulnerability. Here's Hugging Face's version of events, where they explain how they had to use GLM-5.2 because the American "frontier" models "were blocked by the providers' safety guardrails, which cannot distinguish an incident responder from an attacker", lol

Jellyfin's co-founders quit, both burnt out

The two co-founders of the Jellyfin project quit in the last week. One co-founder, Andrew Rabert posted on GitHub that he is experiencing "burnout working within the Jellyfin organization" and "lost almost all motivation to dedicate my time to this" after what appears to be not much love within the Jellyfin community for the desktop app he was leading development of. The other co-founder, Joshua Boniface posted on the Jellyfin forum that "it was just time for a change" and that "hand-off is ongoing and is amicable, and there is good communication lines, so there is little to no risk of a hostile fork or anything of that nature". They've both been working on Jellyfin for around 8 years. Stuff that was fun often doesn't stay fun forever and when there's less fun than good, it's time to walk away. Fingers crossed a strong leader picks up where they left off.

IMAX lost the ability to make 70mm film projectors

The Odyssey at IMAX Melbourne is sold out for months (it's fantastic, go watch it in 1.43:1 if you can! I think the only other cinema is IMAX Sydney in Darling Harbor) and in the USA, the 25 cinemas that can show 1.43:1 aspect ratio 70mm film are also sold out for the next 5 weeks. You'd think this huge demand would prompt IMAX to make more of those glorious projectors so more cinemas can show it as the director intended and cinemas can charge twice as much as a normal ticket for the experience, but according to "sources at IMAX", the parts needed to build the projectors "simply no longer exist", the original design files from the 70s when IMAX started "were never properly maintained" so IMAX has lost the "institutional knowledge" to build a 70mm film projector. That's incredibly sad to me.

Mozilla places multi-account containers in Firefox instead of just an extension

Firefox's Multi-Account Containers extension is super useful and one of the main reasons people like Firefox over Chrome or Safari. Mozilla noticed this and has baked it right into the browser as of Firefox 153. For those unaware, it's like having multiple browsers in one. I personally use it to split between work and personal stuff. You can even set it up so that Facebook, or Google, or whatever gets it own container, so you aren't tracked between these services (e.g: go searching for dick pills on Google, then suddenly get spammed with hair loss programs on Instagram). It's nice to see it become part of the core Firefox offering, so hopefully gets more attention and development.
