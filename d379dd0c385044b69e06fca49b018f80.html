The News

Undocumented changed to infamous Telstra NTP server caused network outage

According to info from a Senate inquiry last week we know why that one specific Microchip Technologies SSU 2000 in Melbourne fucked up, taking Telstra's mobile network with it. It wasn't that nobody updated the firmware, but that "technicians had previously reconfigured the Melbourne server to fix a bug" but did not document those changes, so when the firmware was updated on that fateful day, "it did not respond as expected". I don't know what the undocumented change was, but oof, what a lesson on how documentation is important! ACMA is also looking into it and wishes it had "substantiation" powers, meaning Telstra would have to hand over documents proving it complies with the law, rather than ACMA asking Telstra to hand over only the documents ACMA asks for.

Linus Torvalds claims AI is "a tool, just like other tools we use"

Linus Torvalds has made his opinions on the use of AI/LLM tools on the Linux kernel and in software development very clear - "Linux is not one of those anti-AI projects, and if somebody has issues with that, they can do the open-source thing and fork it. Or just walk away. AI is a tool, just like other tools we use. And it's clearly a useful one". He elaborates further on the kernel mailing list. I am not a programmer so do not have strong opinions about using LLMs to make software, so it's interesting to see what one of the big dogs, both in terms of Linus as a programmer and the Linux kernel as a very important piece of software, thinks of it all. It's a stark contrast to other less privileged or resourced open source projects.

Australian governments releases Statement on Space, a statement about space

Australia's Statement on Space was published today. It is "aimed at supporting the local space industry to deliver the essential services Australians rely on, own more of the value chain, realise benefits beyond space and extend capability into global markets". It outlines a bunch of things Australia's space industry should focus on so that we can have an "enduring space ecosystem that supports Australia's economic resilience and security, is globally competitive, and secures our place in a changing world". This document is the government saying, yeah space is cool, it's important, we see you nerds fiddling with satellites and rockets and we might throw you some extra cash and support one day.

Something I Saw On The Internet

Mullvad makes statement on co-founder's big donation to political party with strong anti-immigration views

Mullvad - a VPN provider I have recommended in the past - published a blog post titled Donation Controversy, a response to one of their co-founders donating a significant amount of money to a political party that "advocates for remigration policies, strong secularism, and assimilationist policies such as immediate closure of the Islamic charter schools" and has a leader saying stuff like "Sweden is "the land for/belongs to the Swedes". I don't know shit from fuck about Swedish politics, but this doesn't sound good. Anyway, Mullvad spends a lot of words trying to pretend it has nothing to do with it and has no opinions on it, but doesn't acknowledge the fact that Mullvad's co-founder got that money he donated from Mullvad's customers, so regardless if Mullvad agrees or disagrees with the donation, it's Mullvad's customers that ultimately funded it.
