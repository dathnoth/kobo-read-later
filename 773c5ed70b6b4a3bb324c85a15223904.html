Aroused by existence

My least favorite part about today, is that we’re not allowed to impressed by anything anymore. Absolutely bizarre.

Like oh, you’re impressed by the fact that we exist? Dude, it’s just a fluke. Oh you’re impressed by consciousness? Dude it’s practically an illusion. Oh, you’re impressed that planet Earth is basically one giant self-regulating organism? Just a weird concoction of chemicals bro.

Oh but you know what is impressive though? GPT 5.5 bro. That shit is real intelligence. It’s like AGI.

Like how did it happen that suddenly the most skeptical, most delirious, most unholy people are suddenly religious zealots? They’ll shutter their curtains at a beautiful sunset but weep at an autocomplete. It’s like now they believe in consciousness and intelligence all of a sudden. Like who is making these machines bro?

Science is supposed to be pretty good at dispelling any notion of magic in the equation. Consciousness and human ingenuity? Just evolution bro. Natural selection. It’s random, it’s undirected. It’s just time and selection bro.

Ok, fine. What about abiogenesis? Where did first life come from? “Ah we don’t know bro. Best not to worry about these things.” Na, you know what, I will, and I will be damn impressed by it. Mattafact, I am so impressed by it, that I’m inclined to basically worship it.

Our solar system? “Na bro just a causal series of events from the Big Bang. You know shit was so hot and spinning so fast and it just happened to congeal like this.” Fine. That the universe exists, period? That anything exists period? “Na bro this is just the singularity bro. Nothing to get aroused by bro.” No you know what, I will be aroused by this. Mattafact, I’m so aroused by this that I’m inclined to worship it.

We dress things up with mundane words and issue citations for anyone getting too excited. It’s like “Stay calm brother. It’s only dumb matter arranging itself into something that feels pain and asks questions about its own existence.”

You can explain every phenomenon using scientific terms. And that’s fine. That’s useful. But the explanation is not the thing itself. An explanation translates. It translates senses into symbols.

I for one am amazed. I am in awe. In absolute wonder. About existence. About us. About intelligence. About our home. About the things we create. Calling it by any other word, like singularity, like evolution, like random, like natural selection—does exactly nothing to dispel the magic for me. It’s all so strangely, bizarrely, unreasonably incredible.

And whatever sense you feel. Whatever you feel when you hear this music play—is more real than any standardized set of symbols we use to describe the music.
