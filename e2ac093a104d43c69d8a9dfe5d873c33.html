Technology is 100% ruining my life

I uh, have an addiction. It’s a common one. It sort of runs in my family. It’s a good way to connect with people. Share stories. You step outside, you take it out, and you just relax for a bit you know. And now, I mean have you seen the stuff they’re coming up with? You can do it anywhere. You do it in the bathroom in the morning. You sneak it out during a nice dinner at a restaurant. No one even notices.

You even do it around the kids now man. My daughter, she’s four, she’s like daddy, why are you always doing this. Does it feel good? I said yeah sweetie, it feels real good. She’s like I can’t wait until I can do that too! And I said not until you’re at least 15 sweetie. I’m not gonna lie, I let her try it a couple times.

Mattafact I’m gonna take one out right now. Oh yeah. That’s the stuff. Just..let..me..open up X real quick. Pull to refresh. Ohhhh yeah.

I’ve tried cigarettes man. They don’t hit as hard. I’m telling you.

I even tried getting myself addicted to cigarettes, because I’m 36, and I need a hobby. I mean have you tried learning piano at this age? I’m 5 years in. I thought I’d be able to play Clair De Lune by now and impress my wife’s friends when they come over. They heard me play man. They’re like, has he considered cigarettes?

Now I’m a YouTuber. I know, I know. I used to be a software developer. But AI took my job. I’ve already been through the 5 stages of grief. I’m at acceptance now.

And being a YouTuber means the numbers own you. There’s an app—YouTube gives you this app that shows your worth as a human being in real time. Casinos are not allowed to do what this app does. A casino legally has to let you leave. This app comes home with you. Every time I refresh, some number changes. Some new comment pops up and tells me I look like Rasputin.

It got to the point where 10% of my job was creating content, and the other 90% was just refreshing shit. A professional refresher. I started to lose my fucking mind.

I was up at 2am googling weird shit on my phone. I was like “who is Rasputin?”

A Russian mystic who was a close friend of the emperor.”

I’m like ok shit well this isn’t bad at all let’s go.

“How did Rasputin die?”

Rasputin had many assassination attempts, surviving poisoning, being shot, and being drowned. He was finally shot 3 times point blank in the head.

And I lost it man. I lost it. I went to the bathroom, plugged in my clipper, and put it to my beard. Do it!! Do it you son of a bitch! Do it.

I hit rock bottom soon after. Which actually is my favorite part of the depression cycle. There’s a strange comfort in knowing you can’t fuck up any more than this.

I wasn’t sure what the fix would be. But I knew where I had to start. This fucking phone. What has it brought me but escapism and distraction. This thief of joy.

Yes, the phone would need to go.

But if I told my family I was going phoneless, they would put me on fucking suicide watch. My mom would come over with a home-cooked meal and try to nurse me back to health.

So I did what any healthy adult does.

I faked my phone’s death.

First I drained the fucker of its soul. Played YouTube at maximum brightness on 5G until it overheated itself to death. It died doing what it loved—killing me.

Step 2: I texted my friends and family from my wife’s phone. I was like, hey guys, it’s me. I lost my phone. Please send your shitty Instagram posts, which I already saw three months ago on X, to my wife, who will describe the content to me against my will while we are driving.

Step 3: Solve all my problems. Right?

Everything was great. I felt light. I felt free. The dawn of a new era.

But my brain had other plans.

The first morning phone-free was super bizarre man. I had a dream where I wake up in my normal world, reach for my phone, open X and just start scrolling. And my brain was like happy about this. It was home. Then I really wake up, and I’m like, what the fuck brain? You fucking sicko. Is this really a freaking addiction? Does my brain think my phone is a drug??

The next few days were rough. My digestive health took a turn.

When’s the last time you took a shit without your phone? Content and caffeine man, it can’t be done without it. So I was like fuck it, let’s grab a book. And the autobiography of Winston Churchill just wasn’t gonna cut it. So I grab what is for some reason the only fiction book on our shelf.

Gone Girl. It was my wife’s.

I go back to the bathroom and resume my morning ritual. And it was painful man. I mean the book too. Fucking trying to wade through the writer’s masturbatory writing about the waft of the oak trees and leaves ruffling and the sun sweltering, and the broad shouldered characters—what the fuck is a broad shoulder?? I have never in my life looked at a man and thought “now THERE’S a broad shoulder.” No one has! It’s a lie writers tell each other!

But then something strange started to happen.

4 days into my sobriety, 4 days into this phone free world, where there is literally nothing else to do but read Gone Girl. And I’m like invested now. I’m like where the fuck is Amy? Did Nick kill her, that broad-shouldered douchebag? I haven’t been this invested in the case of a missing white girl since Nancy Grace came on the air.

I started evangelizing my new religion to the first person I always evangelize my new, totally serious, totally for real this time new religion to.

I’m like honey, honey, you gotta try this. Books.

She doesn’t even look up. She’s like “you’ve been lying on the couch for 6 hours reading a book about something that never happened. How is this making your life any better than being on your phone?”

I get all cocky and shit. I’m like honey, you wouldn’t understand. Us book readers, we’re exercising our brains when we read. You’re on Instagram being a bystander to light waves. Light is just hitting you. You’re a wall. Me, I’m generating worlds. I’m on a bike, you’re in an Uber. She rolls her eyes and reminds me I need to fix her bike chain.

I haven’t fixed the bike chain yet man. It can wait. I’m on a literary journey.

I was like shit I wonder if I could tolerate other boring activities now. I was like a guy bit by a spider wondering if he has spidey senses now. And it worked. I sat down to do something I loved but hated doing: writing. And without my phone, without the nagging feeling of needing to check something, I sat down writing from 8:30pm to midnight. I had never done that before. I didn’t even know that was possible! I was like wait, this was an option!?

I was watching a podcast a few weeks ago. I forget which one. It may have been Rogan. But honestly, it could be any podcast, because everyone says the same thing. One guy will say, you know, back in the nineties, life used to be simple. We didn’t have all these addictions, and social media mental health problems. You had some technology, but it wasn’t like an IV injected straight into your bloodstream. It wasn’t always in your pocket, on your wrist, in your ears. And the other guy, trying to be progressive and smart, says na man. That’s just nostalgia. Life is actually way better now. He’ll give some stat on how standard of living and quality of life is way higher now. How people are more informed, more connected, more in-sync now. How this is all super vital to a well functioning democracy. And the first guy goes yeah, maybe you’re right.

Tech companies will do their annual keynotes about the magic of their technology. The way it brings people together. Faster. Lighter. With more megapixels. And now for a flat monthly fee.

What they don’t show, what they’ll never show, is the addiction. The zombies it creates. The cigarette that hits in the moment but kills you slowly.

The thing that is absolutely, definitely, 100%, ruining your life.

At least cigarettes are honest. At least cigarettes put it on the box.

My daughter, she likes to “watch.” Bluey, Peppa Pig, Doc McStuffins. Always asking us, daddy, can I watch? And every time we say no, she’s like “but I’m soooo bored.” And I got down on her level. I’m like sweetie, when you watch, you’re just a bystander to physics. You want to be pedaling a bicycle, not being a passenger in an Uber.”

She’s like “Fine! Give me a cigarette then.”

That’s my girl.
